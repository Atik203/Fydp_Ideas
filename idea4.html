💡 1. Core Idea

LLMs hallucinate confidently in rare disease diagnosis because their parametric memory is dominated by common conditions. We propose a Neuro-Symbolic Diagnostic Pipeline with a strict division of cognitive labour: the LLM handles natural-language understanding (extracting phenotypic symptoms from clinical text), while a deterministic symbolic reasoner handles diagnostic logic by computing ontological similarity over the Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO) and ORPHANET disease–phenotype associations. The LLM never makes the diagnosis — it only feeds structured input to a provably correct symbolic engine.

🎯 Core Research Contribution

The primary contribution is an end-to-end pipeline from free-text clinical narrative to ranked rare disease diagnoses with zero reasoning-layer hallucination — a property provably absent in pure-LLM diagnostics. Secondary contribution: an improved clinical phenotype extraction model handling negation, uncertainty qualifiers, and family history mentions.

🔍 2. Problem Statement

2.1 The Rare Disease Diagnostic Crisis

Rare diseases collectively affect ~300 million people globally. The average time to diagnosis is 5–7 years (Eurordis, 2023). LLMs fail on rare conditions because:

  • Frequency bias: Training data is heavily skewed toward common diseases — a rare condition with 50 documented cases has ~50× less training signal than a common one.
  • Phenotypic mimicry: Rare disease presentations often overlap with common conditions; LLMs pick the statistically more likely common condition.
  • Hallucination without calibration: Singhal et al. (2023) showed Med-PaLM 2 achieves only ~45% accuracy on rare disease vignettes vs. ~86% on common conditions — a 41-point gap that fine-tuning alone cannot close.

2.2 Why This Architecture Works

Clinical geneticists already use ontology-based reasoning tools (Phenomizer, OMIM) — the problem is they require manual HPO code entry. The real gap is a natural language front-end for these tools, not a replacement for their reasoning engine. Our architecture provides exactly that.

⚠ Explicit Research Gap

No published end-to-end system bridges free-text clinical narratives → structured HPO extraction → symbolic ontological reasoning → ranked differential diagnosis with explainable HPO paths. The two halves exist (PhenoBERT for extraction; Phenomizer for reasoning) but have never been integrated into a unified, evaluated pipeline.

🏗️ 4. Proposed 3-Stage Neuro-Symbolic Pipeline

Stage 1 — Neural Phenotype Extraction

An LLM (Llama-3-8B, LoRA fine-tuned on RAMEDIS vignettes) extracts a structured phenotype profile from the clinical narrative, classifying each symptom mention as:

  • Positive: "bilateral cataracts" → HP:0000518
  • Negated: "no hepatomegaly" → ¬HP:0002240
  • Uncertain: "possible developmental delay" → HP:0001263, conf=0.5
  • Family history: "mother had similar symptoms" → HP:XXXX, source=family

Stage 2 — Symbolic Diagnostic Reasoning (Resnik Similarity over HPO DAG)

The extracted HPO term set is passed to a deterministic graph algorithm over the HPO Directed Acyclic Graph (17,000 terms; 30,000 edges) + ORPHANET matrix (~100K disease–phenotype pairs):

sim(P_patient, P_disease) = (1/|P|) Σ maxj IC(MICA(p_i, p_j))

Where IC(t) = −log(p(t)) (Information Content) and MICA = Most Informative Common Ancestor in the HPO DAG. Negated phenotypes apply a penalty term λ·IC(negated_term). This step is fully deterministic — identical input always produces identical ranking.

Stage 3 — Neural Explanation Generation

The LLM receives the top-5 ranked diseases with their ontological similarity paths and generates a human-readable rationale explicitly citing HPO ancestry: "EDS ranked #1 because joint hypermobility (HP:0001382) and skin hyperextensibility (HP:0001030) share a common ancestor 'Connective tissue abnormality' (HP:0003549) with IC=4.2."

⚠ Risks & Mitigations
  • EHR data access (CRITICAL — INFEASIBLE): Real patient records require IRB approval (6+ months) and HIPAA compliance. Mitigation: Use ONLY public datasets (RAMEDIS, published case reports). Frame as proof-of-concept on standardized vignettes, not a clinical study.
  • Venue for publication (HIGH RISK): JAMIA/JBHI require clinical validation. Mitigation: Target BioNLP Shared Task @ ACL or ClinicalNLP workshop as first venue. Full journal submission as PhD Year 2 project.
  • HPO extraction quality: Negation and family history are hard for LLMs. Mitigation: RAMEDIS vignettes are relatively clean; negation handled by explicit classifier head on top of LLM embeddings.
