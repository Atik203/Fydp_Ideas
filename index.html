This document is a formal research proposal submitted to my FYDP supervisor for the academic year 2026–2027. ⚖️ 2. Academic Evaluation Framework

Every research direction in this proposal is evaluated against the following five axes, each reflecting a critical dimension of a publishable, feasible FYDP project. Scoring is on a 1–5 scale assessed by the author; the supervisor is invited to re-score any dimension before a final direction is selected.

Axis 1 — Novelty

Does the idea close an explicit, citable research gap? Does it contribute a new algorithm, architecture, or methodology — not just a new application of known methods?

Axis 2 — Methodological Rigor

Can the evaluation plan yield statistically significant, reproducible results with clear ablation studies, multiple baselines, and proper confidence intervals?

Axis 3 — Engineering Complexity

Does the system address open-ended, ill-defined problems meeting Washington Accord graduate-level standards? Is it more than a pipeline of existing APIs?

Axis 4 — Feasibility

Is the scope honestly achievable in 12 months with ≤ 2×A100 compute, open-weight models, and publicly available datasets — without EHR, legal, or IRB dependencies?

Axis 5 — Publication Readiness

Could a well-executed version realistically survive Q1 peer review at ACL, EMNLP, NeurIPS, or ICLR? Does it tell a clean story with a falsifiable hypothesis and a meaningful baseline gap?
