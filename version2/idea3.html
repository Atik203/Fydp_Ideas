Core Idea

What This Project Proposes

We propose TKD-RAG — a Temporal Knowledge Decay-aware Retrieval-Augmented Generation framework that introduces a Temporal Validity Score (TVS) to weight retrieved documents by their temporal relevance and a Dynamic Knowledge Conflict Resolver (DKCR) to detect and resolve conflicts between an LLM's frozen parametric memory and freshly retrieved documents. The central insight is that not all retrieved documents are equally valid over time — a 2019 drug dosage guideline retrieved for a 2025 query must be treated differently from a 2025 update. TVS formalizes this intuition with domain-specific decay models (medical decay is faster than geological decay). The TempoConflict benchmark (1,800 temporally conflicting QA pairs) serves as a community contribution.

EMNLP is the realistic ceiling venue — Nature MI claims require substantially more contribution than this project alone.

Problem Statement
  • Medical NLP systems trained through 2022 miss 2024 WHO drug guidelines — direct patient safety risk in clinical decision support
  • Legal NLP misses new case law, amended statutes, regulatory changes — advice based on superseded law creates liability
  • Financial NLP has outdated company profiles, leadership changes, market conditions — generates incorrect investment signals
  • Full LLM retraining costs $4M–$100M — not viable for continuous knowledge updates. Periodic retraining loses old knowledge (catastrophic forgetting).
  • Standard RAG (Lewis et al., 2020) retrieves fresh text but ignores: (a) temporal validity of retrieved documents, (b) conflicts between retrieved content and parametric memory. Conflicting knowledge is silently averaged or dropped.
  • Engineering complexity: Requires formal definition of temporal validity, domain-specific decay modeling, conflict detection between neural memory and retrieved text, and resolution strategy — each a non-trivial research contribution
📚
Existing Solutions & Limitations
  • RAG (Lewis et al., NeurIPS 2020): Retrieves fresh text but treats all retrieved documents as equally valid regardless of publication date. No temporal validity weighting. No conflict detection with parametric memory.
  • TempLAMA (Dhingra et al., 2022): Benchmark for temporal knowledge probing — evaluates retrieval accuracy but does not provide conflict resolution. Measures the problem, not the solution.
  • TemporalWiki (Jang et al., 2022): Temporal knowledge graph dataset — exists but has no conflict resolver system built on it. Dataset only.
  • ROME / MEMIT (Meng et al., 2022; 2023): Knowledge editing approaches — modify model weights directly. Does not scale to continuous temporal drift. One-off edits, not dynamic updates. Catastrophic interference with related facts.
  • T-RAG variants: Time-aware retrieval in isolated papers — none provides a unified temporal validity metric + conflict resolver + benchmark combination.
  • Periodic retraining: Standard industry approach. Extremely expensive, not continuous, creates sharp knowledge discontinuities at retraining boundaries.
🔬
Research Gap
  • Gap 1: No formal metric exists to score "how stale" a retrieved document is relative to a query in a domain-aware way. TVS does not exist in any form in the RAG literature.
  • Gap 2: No framework detects and explicitly resolves conflicts between LLM parametric memory and retrieved documents. Current RAG either ignores parametric knowledge or blindly overrides it — no principled conflict resolution exists.
  • Gap 3: Domain-specific temporal decay rates have never been empirically characterized. Medical knowledge has a different half-life than geological knowledge — but this has never been measured and modeled in NLP.
  • Gap 4: No benchmark for temporal knowledge conflict resolution in NLP exists. TempoConflict does not exist. TempLAMA is a probing benchmark, not a conflict resolution benchmark.
  • Gap 5: Temporal reasoning in LLMs is well-studied at the entity level (when did X happen) but not at the validity level (is X still true?). The distinction is critical for applied NLP.
  • Gap 6: The RAG literature does not distinguish between "newly retrieved" and "more valid" — a 2025 blog post is newer than a 2020 systematic review but less authoritative. TVS must jointly model recency AND authority.

System Architecture & Pipeline
INPUT: Time-sensitive query
       e.g., "What is the current recommended first-line treatment for Type 2 diabetes?"
       Query timestamp: 2025-01-15

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
STAGE 1: RETRIEVAL WITH TEMPORAL METADATA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Retrieve: Top-K documents {D_1, ..., D_K}
  Metadata: {pub_date, source_type, journal_IF, domain}
  Domain classifier: medical | legal | financial | general

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
STAGE 2: TEMPORAL VALIDITY SCORE (TVS)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  For each document D_i:

  Recency Component (R_i):
    Δt = query_date - pub_date(D_i)     [days]
    λ_domain:
      Medical:   λ_med   = 0.003  [fast decay: ~3yr half-life]
      Legal:     λ_legal = 0.001  [medium: ~7yr half-life]
      Financial: λ_fin   = 0.01   [very fast: ~3mo half-life]
      General:   λ_gen   = 0.0003 [slow: ~20yr half-life]
    R_i = exp(-λ_domain × Δt)          [exponential decay]

  Authority Component (A_i):
    A_i = α·JIF_percentile(D_i) + β·citation_score(D_i)
          [JIF = journal impact factor; citation normalized]

  Temporal Validity Score:
    TVS(D_i) = γ·R_i + (1-γ)·A_i      [γ learned on val set]
    Range: [0.0, 1.0]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
STAGE 3: CONFLICT DETECTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Parametric Memory Probe:
    Use LLM zero-shot to express its stored belief:
    "What do you know about [query entity]?"
    → Extract parametric assertion P_memory

  NLI-based Conflict Classifier (DeBERTa-v3 fine-tuned):
    Input: (P_memory, D_i_relevant_passage)
    Label: [No Conflict | Soft Conflict | Hard Conflict]

  Conflict scores aggregated across all retrieved D_i

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
STAGE 4: DYNAMIC KNOWLEDGE CONFLICT RESOLVER (DKCR)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Resolution Strategy (TVS-weighted):

  CASE A: No Conflict (most documents, low TVS variance)
    → Standard RAG generation, no special handling

  CASE B: Soft Conflict (partial disagreement)
    → Weighted fusion: output = αP_memory + (1-α)D_high_TVS
    → α = f(TVS_max, conflict_severity)
    → "According to [source, YEAR], ... This updated..."

  CASE C: Hard Conflict (direct contradiction)
    → Override: prioritize document with highest TVS(D_i)
    → Temporal reasoning chain injected into prompt:
      "As of [DATE], [ENTITY] had [PROPERTY].
       As of [NEW_DATE], [ENTITY] has [NEW_PROPERTY]."
    → Explicit acknowledgment: "This conflicts with older guidance..."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
STAGE 5: TEMPORAL REASONING CHAIN GENERATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Structured temporal chain injected as context:
  "[2019 WHO guideline: Metformin + sulfonylurea]
   [2022 ADA guideline: Metformin + SGLT-2 inhibitor]
   [2024 NICE guideline: Metformin + GLP-1 agonist for BMI>30]"
  → LLM generates answer with explicit temporal attribution

OUTPUT:
  Answer:         Contextualized, temporally-aware response
  Conflict Type:  [No Conflict | Soft | Hard]
  TVS Scores:     Per-document validity scores (interpretable)
  Temporal Chain: "Knowledge updated: 2019→2022→2024"
  Confidence:     Based on TVS of highest-scoring supporting doc

MODELS:    DeBERTa-v3 (conflict classifier), T5-large (generator)
           GPT-4 / Llama-3 (LLM backbone for final generation)
DATASETS:  TempLAMA, TemporalWiki, TempoConflict (custom)
COMPUTE:   1× A6000 or RTX 3090 (DeBERTa fine-tuning)

Risks & Mitigations
  • Risk 1 (MEDIUM): TVS decay parameter λ_domain must be empirically validated, not assumed. Without validation, TVS is arbitrary. Mitigation: Measure knowledge half-lives empirically from Wikipedia revision histories and PubMed update patterns. Ground λ in data, not intuition.
  • Risk 2 (MEDIUM): TempoConflict construction requires careful design — naive automatic extraction produces noisy conflicts. Mitigation: Use Wikipedia revision pairs with reverted edits as a clean signal of temporal knowledge change. Validate 200 pairs manually.
  • Risk 3 (LOW): Conflict detection on soft conflicts is inherently ambiguous. Mitigation: Three-class labeling (None/Soft/Hard) with inter-annotator agreement gate (κ > 0.70 required before proceeding).
  • Risk 4 (LOW): Limited expert evaluators for TVS validation. Mitigation: Medical domain: recruit through university hospital collaboration. Legal domain: law faculty. Financial: economics department. Contact in Month 3, not Month 9.
  • Venue ceiling (ACKNOWLEDGED): EMNLP is the realistic target. Nature Machine Intelligence requires a substantially larger scope — do not overclaim in the introduction.
